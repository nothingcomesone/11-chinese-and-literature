④ 答题 Question 1 (10 marks)

(a)1 分

根据对话开头，最近年轻人中出现了什么现象？

根據對話開頭，最近年輕人中出現了什麼現象？

According to the beginning of the conversation, what recent phenomenon among young people is mentioned?

评分标准：1分——准确指出“越来越多年轻人重新关注传统文化”这一现象（如穿汉服、逛博物馆、报国学班，超过六成）。
范文：More and more young people are re-engaging with traditional culture — over sixty per cent according to a recent survey, with some wearing hanfu to visit museums and others enrolling in classes on traditional learning.
(b)3 分

对话中，主持人对传统价值观提出了哪些疑虑？

對話中，主持人對傳統價值觀提出了哪些疑慮？

What doubts about traditional values does the host raise in the conversation?

评分标准：3分——充分理解主持人的疑虑，归纳出三点并表述清楚；2分——有一定理解，归纳不全；1分——提供相关信息。
范文：The host raises three doubts. First, she worries that traditional values such as filial piety and courteous deference were formed hundreds or even thousands of years ago, and so may be out of step with modern life. Second, she fears that some of these values may constrain young people, citing "blind filial piety" as an example. Third, she questions whether being honest and yielding to others in today's fiercely competitive society would put young people at a disadvantage.
(c)6 分

林教授在对话中是如何讲解传统价值观对现代年轻人的意义的？联系对话内容和语言特色来回答问题。

林教授在對話中是如何講解傳統價值觀對現代年輕人的意義的？聯繫對話內容和語言特色來回答問題。

How does Professor Lin explain the relevance of traditional values to young people today? In your answer, refer to both the content and the language features used in the conversation.

Technique — 报技巧名
反问？数据？比喻？排比？引用？
Example — 引对话原文（英译）
Effect — 说效果（说服了谁、怎么说服）
评分标准：6分——全面理解林教授如何讲解传统价值观的现代意义，内容与语言特色引用充分；5分——透彻理解，引用详细；4分——合理理解，能兼及内容与语言特色；2–3分——有一定理解，仅涉及其一或两者都有限；1分——提供相关信息。
范文（约300词）：Professor Lin explains the modern relevance of traditional values through three angles, layering persuasive language features throughout. Firstly, he uses a personal anecdote — having worked away from home for thirty years, he telephones his mother every Sunday night, and "this phone call makes her happier than the money I send home". Combined with the contrast between extended families of the past and today's nuclear families and elderly people living alone, this illustrates that while the form of filial piety has changed, the heart of respect and companionship is "more precious than ever". He reinforces this with the proverb "of all virtues, filial piety comes first", clarifying that it means "not forgetting one's roots" rather than obedience. Secondly, he cites statistics — a survey of five hundred companies in which eighty-five per cent of employers ranked integrity first among hiring criteria — to substantiate that traditional virtues are practical assets. The parallelism "they teach us honesty, teach us respect for others, teach us constant self-improvement" builds rhythmic momentum, and the famous quotation of Cai Yuanpei urging students to "refine moral conduct" lends historical and cultural authority to his argument. Finally, he poses the rhetorical question "surely we are not bringing back blind filial piety?" to concede that not everything old deserves reviving, before offering the metaphor of a great tree — "keep the roots firm, let the branches renew" — which vividly frames his conclusion that young people should "absorb the essence and discard the dross". These techniques progressively move the initially sceptical host to agreement, so much so that she resolves to visit her grandmother that weekend.
给自己核分（微观清单）：
  • 内容点：□ 现象（六成关注传统文化） □ 孝的形式变内核更珍贵 □ 传统品德=职场竞争力 □ 取其精华去其糟粕（各≈1分）
  • 技巧循环（技巧+例子+效果，每组≈1.5分）：□ 个人经历 □ 对比 □ 谚语“百善孝为先” □ 数据85% □ 排比“教我们……” □ 引用蔡元培“砥砺德行” □ 反问 □ 比喻“大树/根/新芽” □ 成语
  • □ 语言通顺（0.5分）　只报技巧名、不给例子不讲效果 = 每处掉一半分。
